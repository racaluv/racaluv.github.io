SILENT SANCTUARY


A 5-piece Filipino band. They entered the OPM (Original Pilipino Music) scene in 2006 with their songs “Rebound”, “Ikaw Lamang”, “Summer Song”, “Kundiman”, and “Ingat ka”. In their second studio album called Mistaken for Granted, the album's first single is called "Hay Naku" and the second one is called "Hiling". Silent Sanctuary was formed in 2001 with just three founding members, Norman Dellosa (vocals, guitars), Paolo Legaspi (bass guitar), and Allen Calixto (drums). As a growing band, they experimented in their music by mixing in classical instruments to make a unique sound, adding the fourth member, Norman's high school classmate Anjo Inacay (cellist) to the line-up. In 2002 they were asked to guest in UnTV's In the Raw where Anjo asked Jett Ramirez to create string arrangements for their performance.

ABOUT ROTARACT CLUB

Rotaract Club of Virac is a community-based service club for young men and women ages 18 to 30 who are dedicated to community and international service. It has opened a venue for young Catandunganons who want to be part of positive changes in the community, to share ideas, to build new friendship, and to look at the world’s challenges in a new way. It has been honing potential leaders of our community and has continually undertaken numerous projects and programs which have helped our less fortunate fellow Catandunganons for the past six Rotaract Years since its creation.

The Rotaract Club of Virac, a service, leadership, and community service organization and a proud member of the Rotary International, will be presenting RAC-a-LUV II: A Valentine Benefit Concert featuring Silent Sanctuary with front act performances from local bands (Heretic, A-Sharp, Higher Ground & Good Vibes) on 13 February 2015.

The main objective of this concert is to generate funds for Rotaract Club’s community service projects as well as to promote and develop our local and home-grown talents.

Last year’s concert was a success by all measure, from the number of attendees with a record of more than 3,000 attendees, to the number of local talents who have performed, and most importantly the funds generated enough to sponsor a rehabilitation of a Day Care Center not to mention all other activities such as feedings, tsinelas drive and other quality service projects for the less fortunate not just in Virac but in other towns as well.
